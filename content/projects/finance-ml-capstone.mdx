## Problem

Most personal-finance apps tell you what already happened. I wanted one that
could answer "given my last 12 months of spending, will I overshoot rent next
month?" before it actually happens.

## What I built

A layered FastAPI backend that ingests bank-CSV imports, normalizes them into
budgets, goals, and transactions, and serves a Flutter mobile client.

- Forecasting model: gradient-boosted regressor trained per-user on rolling
  category-level windows.
- Backend: FastAPI with SQLAlchemy + Postgres, layered into routes / services /
  repositories so each domain (budgets, goals, transactions) follows the same
  shape.
- Frontend: Flutter app with a custom chart widget for projected vs. actual
  spend.

<Callout title="Why per-user models" variant="info">
  Global models smoothed away the patterns that mattered for individuals
  (e.g. someone who buys groceries once a month). Per-user models with very
  light hyper-parameter search were both more accurate and easier to debug.
</Callout>

## Impact

- Personal forecast accuracy within ~8% of actual monthly spend across 6 test
  users.
- Cut the time to onboard a new domain (e.g. "savings goal") from a day to
  about an hour by templating the layered structure.
- Capstone demo, used as a reference for course conventions afterward.
